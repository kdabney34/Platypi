About The Duck-Billed Platypus

The Duck-Billed Platypus (Ornithorhynchus anatinus) is one interesting creature. Its conservation status is currently Not Extinct.

It is a member of the rare group of animals called "Monotremes" which was the earliest offshoot from the Mammalian family tree.

Qualities

  • Venomous
  • Egg-laying
  • Otter-like body
  • Duck-like feet

Fascinating Genome

The platypus produces egg yolk proteins shared only with reptiles and fish. Additionally, it also has a set of mammalian genes responsible for lactation.

Skilled Hunter

In addition to electroreceptors in their bills, the platypus uses chemical cues when navigating and pursuing their prey underwater
